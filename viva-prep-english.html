🕐 Telling the Time & Numbers Ch. 2
✓
Clock Expressions
O'clock = exact hour (:00). Half past = 30 mins after. Quarter past = 15 mins after. Quarter to = 15 mins before next hour. Five/ten/twenty past/to for other times. Nearly = almost the next hour. Just after = just past the hour.
3:00 = three o'clock7:30 = half past seven6:45 = quarter to seven9:50 = ten to ten
✓
Numbers in Words
21–99: hyphen (twenty-one). 100 = one hundred. 1,000 = one thousand. British English uses 'and' between hundreds and smaller numbers (one hundred and fifty). 'About' = approximately. 1,000,000 = one million.
1,250 = one thousand two hundred and fiftyabout = approximate

Daily Routine Phrases & Order
Morning order: get up → have a shower → get dressed → have breakfast → leave home. Evening: finish work → come home → have dinner → go to bed. Key verbs: have (breakfast/shower/dinner), go (to work/shopping/for a walk), leave (home), finish (work).

Frequency Adverbs & Expressions
Always (100%) → usually/normally → often → sometimes → rarely → never (0%). Position: BEFORE main verb, AFTER 'be'. Once a week (1×), twice a week (2×). Go out = leave for leisure. Stay in = remain at home. Weekend = Saturday and Sunday.

📐 Grammar: Present Simple & Simple Past Handout
✓
Present Simple
Use: daily habits, likes/dislikes, general truths. Positive: I/You/We/They + base verb. He/She/It + verb+s (she eats, he lives). Negative: don't (I/You/We/They) or doesn't (He/She/It) + base verb. Questions: Do/Does + subject + base verb? 'Does he eat?' NOT 'Does he eats?'
she eat✗ → she eats✓doesn't eat✓ NOT doesn't eats✗
✓
Present Continuous
Use: actions happening RIGHT NOW. Form: am/is/are + verb-ing. I am cooking. She is watching. They are reading. Questions: Is she going? Are they waiting? Negative contractions: I'm not, He isn't / He's not, They aren't / They're not.
am/is/are + -ingIs she cooking? ✓
✓
Simple Past
Use: completed past actions. Regular: verb + -ed (walked, danced, climbed). Irregular: sleep→slept, run→ran, ride→rode, sit→sat. Negative: didn't + BASE verb (NOT past form). 'She didn't walk' ✓ NOT 'She didn't walked' ✗. Questions: Did + subject + base verb?
didn't + base verb alwaysDid he sleep?✓ NOT slept✗

🗣️ Communication Skills Topic 07
✓
Definition & 5 Elements
Communication = exchanging ideas, feelings, thoughts, and information between two or more people. Effective communication = receiver understands clearly without confusion. 5 elements: Sender, Message, Receiver, Medium, Feedback. Without feedback = communication is incomplete.
5 elementsno feedback = incomplete
✓
The 7 C's of Communication
Clear (no ambiguity) · Concise (brief, no waffle) · Concrete (specific, facts-based) · Correct (accurate grammar, right facts) · Coherent (logically organised) · Complete (all information included) · Courteous (polite, respectful).
ClearConciseConcreteCorrectCoherentCompleteCourteous
✓
Verbal vs Non-Verbal
Verbal = spoken or written words (conversations, emails, presentations). Non-verbal = body language, facial expressions, tone, gestures — up to 90% of communication. Non-verbal is often unconscious. Types: Facial expressions, Body language, Gestures, Eye contact (Gaze), Paralinguistics (tone/pitch/speed), Haptics (touch), Proxemics (personal space), Chronemics (use of time).
non-verbal = up to 90%often unconscious
✓
Body Language: Positive vs Negative
Positive: upright posture, open hands, friendly smile, firm handshake, good eye contact, leaning forward, nodding. Negative: crossed arms (defensive), avoiding eye contact (nervous/dishonest), slouching (disinterest), weak handshake (insecurity), looking down (lack of confidence). First impressions are formed through body language before words.
crossed arms = defensivenod = agreement
✓
Barriers & How to Remove Them
Barriers: Noise (physical), Language barrier (linguistic), Lack of attention/Poor listening, Wrong body language, Stress/Anger (emotional), Cultural differences. Remove: speak slowly and clearly, use simple words, stay calm, listen carefully, use positive body language, avoid distractions, use visual aids, repeat key information.
physical / emotional / linguistic / psychological

MBTI — 4 Dichotomies & 16 Types
MBTI = Myers-Briggs Type Indicator. Developed by Isabel Briggs Myers & Katherine Cook Briggs. Inspired by Carl Jung. 16 personality types. Does NOT measure intelligence. No right or wrong answers — self-reflective tool. Purpose: enhance self-awareness and improve communication. 4 dichotomies: E/I (energy direction) · S/N (information) · T/F (decisions) · J/P (organisation).
E/I · S/N · T/F · J/P16 typesnot intelligence
✓
MBTI Dichotomies in Detail
E vs I: where you direct energy — outward (people/environment) vs inward (thoughts/reflection). S vs N: how you take in info — concrete facts/present vs patterns/possibilities/big picture. T vs F: how you make decisions — logic/objectivity vs personal values/impact on others. J vs P: how you organise — structured/planned vs flexible/spontaneous.
E=outward / I=inwardS=facts / N=patternsT=logic / F=valuesJ=planned / P=flexible
🎯 Values, Work Ethics, Self-Awareness & SMART Goals GPDP 02
✓
Values & Work Ethics
Values = fundamental beliefs that influence attitude and determine what is meaningful and important — the things we consider good, desirable, worthwhile. Work ethics = set of values and attitudes individuals bring to jobs, enabling decisions with integrity. Strong work ethics in hospitality: honesty, responsibility, quality, service, discipline, humility, teamwork. Opposite of ethical: theft, violence, dishonesty, neglect.
7 work ethicstheft/violence/dishonesty = unethical
✓
Self-Awareness
Self-awareness = ability to understand your own thoughts, emotions, strengths, and areas for improvement. It is a HABIT that can be developed through regular reflection and practice. Strengths = positive traits you perform well (organised, confident, team player). Weaknesses = areas needing improvement (poor time management, public speaking). Self-awareness helps answer interview questions confidently.
habit = can be developedstrengths vs weaknesses
✓
Goal Setting & SMART
Goal setting = deciding what you want, breaking into steps, taking action. Without goals = lose focus (like a trip without a destination). 5 key questions: What can I do now? What do I want? What should I do? Who can help? How will I know I've succeeded? SMART: Specific (clear/precise) · Measurable (trackable indicators) · Achievable (realistic) · Relevant (career-aligned) · Time-based (clear deadline).
S·M·A·R·T5 key goal questions
