🍲 Stocks, Soups & Broths 6 points
✓
White Stock (Fond Blanc)
Raw bones + cold water + mirepoix + bouquet garni. Blanch bones first. Simmer gently — never boil. Chicken: 3–4 hrs. Veal/beef: 6–8 hrs. Never season.
raw bonescold water startno seasoning
✓
Brown Stock (Fond Brun)
Roast bones + caramelise mirepoix first (Maillard reaction). Deep colour and rich flavour. Base for demi-glace and all classical brown sauces.
roasted bonesMaillard reactiondemi-glace base
✓
Fish Stock (Fumet)
Fish bones + white wine + aromatics. 20–30 minutes ONLY — longer makes it bitter. Base for fish velouté and seafood sauces.
20–30 mins maxwhite winefish velouté base
✓
Consommé
Crystal-clear clarified soup. Cloudy stock + clearmeat raft (lean mince + egg white + mirepoix + acid). Raft traps all impurities, then strained.
clearmeat raftegg white clarificationcrystal clear
✓
Soup Categories
Clear (consommé, broth) · Cream (velouté base + cream liaison) · Purée (blended starchy veg, no roux) · Bisque (crustacean cream soup) · National (French Onion, Gazpacho, Vichyssoise).

Moist Heat Methods

Heat Transfer: Conduction, Convection, Radiation
Conduction = direct contact (pan to food). Convection = fluid movement (oven air, boiling water). Radiation = electromagnetic waves (salamander, charcoal).

Combination = sear (dry heat) then braise (moist heat). Converts collagen to gelatin in tough cuts. Sous vide = vacuum-sealed food in precisely controlled water bath temperature.

Primal Cuts Overview
Large sections divided first from the carcass. Forequarter (chuck, brisket, rib, shank) = tough, more collagen, slow cook. Hindquarter (loin, sirloin, rump, round) = tender, less collagen, fast cook.

Tender Cuts (Hindquarter)
Fillet/Tenderloin = most tender, beneath backbone, least used muscle. Sirloin = between loin and rump, fat cap, grill or pan-fry. Rump = firmer, more flavour. Rib eye = high marbling.

Chef's Knife (Cook's Knife)
20–25 cm. General purpose — chopping, slicing, dicing, mincing. Most used knife in any kitchen. Broad tapered blade. Western style uses rocking chop.

Paring Knife
6–10 cm. Small precision knife for peeling, trimming, deveining prawns, and intricate work. Often used in-hand rather than on a board.

Boning Knife
Narrow, pointed blade. Stiff = beef/pork. Flexible = poultry/fish. Works close to bone, through joints and cartilage to separate meat cleanly.

Bread Knife (Serrated)
Long serrated edge. Teeth grip the crust then saw through soft crumb without compressing it. Also for tomatoes and soft fruit. Cannot be resharpened easily.

Filleting Knife
Long, thin, very flexible blade. Runs along fish spine to separate flesh from bone cleanly with minimal waste. Also removes skin from fillets.

Santoku
Japanese. 16–18 cm. Shorter, wider, sheep's foot tip (rounded). Often granton edge. Push-cut technique, not rocking. Versatile: slice, dice, mince.

Japanese. Rectangular blade, straight edge, blunt tip. Designed exclusively for vegetables. Straight push-cut — full edge contacts board simultaneously for clean, even cuts.

✓
Steel Types & Hardness
High-carbon steel = harder, sharper, rusts if not dried. Stainless = corrosion resistant, softer. HRC scale: Western 52–58, Japanese 60–66+. Higher HRC = sharper, holds edge longer, but more brittle.
HRC = hardnessJapanese harderdry carbon steel
✓
Forged vs Stamped
Forged = heated steel shaped under pressure → denser, better balance, natural bolster, superior performance. Stamped = cut from flat sheet → lighter, cheaper, no natural bolster. Forged preferred professionally.
forged = superiorstamped = lighterbolster = forged indicator
✓
Honing vs Sharpening
Honing steel = realigns rolled edge, no metal removed. Done frequently. Angle: 15–20° Western, 10–15° Japanese. Whetstone = removes metal to create new edge. Coarse grit first (repair), then fine (polish).
honing = realignwhetstone = sharpen15–20° Western
✓
Sharpness Test & Edge Geometry
Light test: hold edge to light — sharp edge reflects NO light. Single bevel (one side, Japanese) = sharper but fragile. Double bevel (both sides, Western) = durable. Granton = oval recesses on flat, prevents sticking.
light testsingle vs double bevelgranton edge

✓
Handwashing Procedure & Timing
Warm water + soap, scrub ALL surfaces (palms, backs, between fingers, under nails, wrists) for 20 seconds minimum. Dry with single-use paper towel. Must wash: before food, after raw meat, after toilet, after touching face/hair/phone/waste.
20 seconds minimumsingle-use towelall surfaces

Uniform & Grooming Standards
Clean uniform every shift. Hair fully covered. Nails short, clean, unvarnished. No jewellery (plain wedding band exception). No strong perfume. Blue waterproof plasters on cuts. No eating/smoking/drinking in prep areas.

Illness Exclusion Rules
Vomiting or diarrhoea = stay home. Return only 48 hours after ALL symptoms resolved. Infected wounds, boils, weeping skin = report to supervisor. Norovirus sheds even after feeling better.

Clean As You Go (CAYG)
Clean workstations, equipment, and spills immediately as they occur — not only at end of service. Prevents bacteria multiplying on soiled surfaces, reduces pest attraction, maintains food safety throughout the shift.

FAT TOM — Bacterial Growth Conditions
Food (protein/carb rich) · Acidity (pH 4.6–7.5 optimal) · Temperature (5–63°C) · Time (2+ hrs in danger zone) · Oxygen (most bacteria need it) · Moisture (high water activity). Control these = control bacteria.
F-A-T-T-O-MpH 4.6–7.5water activity
✓
Safe Cooling & Thawing
Cool: ice bath, shallow containers. From 60°C → below 21°C in 2 hours, then to 5°C in 4 more hours. Thaw: refrigerator (safest), cold running water, or microwave if cooking immediately. NEVER at room temperature.
2+4 hour cooling ruleice bathnever room temp thaw
✓
HACCP & Temperature Monitoring
Temperature is a Critical Control Point (CCP) in HACCP. Fridges and freezers logged minimum twice daily. Probe thermometer calibrated in ice water (0°C) and boiling water (100°C). Sanitise probe between uses.
CCPlog twice dailycalibrate probe
✓
Refrigerator Storage Order
Top to bottom: Ready-to-eat foods → Whole fish → Whole cuts of beef/pork → Minced meat → Raw poultry (bottom). Raw poultry on bottom — highest contamination risk if juices drip. FIFO and FEFO for rotation.
